# Adelos — What It Does

> **I'm back. It's me. You don't know me — but you do know that it is me.**

This is the plain-language guide. No cryptography. If you want the technical detail, see the [technical document](claims-technical.md).

---

## The idea in one breath

Adelos lets you **prove things about yourself without saying who you are.**

Prove you're an adult. Prove you're a qualified nurse. Prove you live in a particular country. Prove you're a real, unique person and not a bot — and disclose *only* that, to *only* the people you choose, with *nobody* keeping a record of when or where you did it.

It is a small device you build yourself. It costs a few pounds. No company runs it. No government issues it. Nobody can switch it off.

## Why this matters

The world is moving toward **centralised digital identity** — a single official ID that you show to everyone, run by one authority. That authority can watch everywhere you use it, link all your activity together, and revoke you at will.

Adelos is the opposite. You prove what you need to prove, to whoever needs it, and the proof reveals nothing else. No central watcher. No master list. No off switch.

## What "proof without identity" actually means

When you use Adelos, two things are true at once:

- The other side **knows it's genuinely you** — the same person as last time, not an impostor or a bot.
- The other side **does not know who you are** — no name, no birthdate, no address.

That sounds contradictory. It isn't. The device proves *continuity* (it's the same one) without proving *identity* (who). That's the whole trick.

## A real example: proving you're an adult

Say you want to buy something age-restricted online, but you don't want to hand the shop your passport, your name, or your date of birth.

Here's how it works with Adelos:

1. **A service you already use vouches for you — once.** Imagine a social media platform you've used for years. From your history, it can tell you're an adult. It issues a one-time, signed statement: *"The person controlling this device is an adult."* It signs that against your device, **not against your name.** Your name stays with the platform and never travels.

2. **You carry that proof on your device.** It's locked to your fingerprint. Only you can use it.

3. **You prove it to the shop.** The shop sees: *"A source I trust confirms this controller is an adult, and they're genuinely here right now."* It does **not** see your name, your birthdate, or which platform vouched for you. The platform is never contacted and never finds out you went shopping.

The shop gets exactly what it needs — "yes, adult, verified, real person" — and nothing more.

## What you can prove

- **Age** — over 18, over 21, without revealing your birthdate.
- **Qualifications** — you hold a licence or degree, without revealing your name or institution.
- **Where you're entitled to services** — your country or region, without revealing your address.
- **Membership** — you're a paid member or subscriber, without an account.
- **That you're one real person** — anti-bot, anti-sybil, without identifying you.

In every case: you disclose the single fact, nothing else.

## What stays private

- Your name.
- Your date of birth, address, and other details.
- **Where and when you use your proofs** — no central log exists.
- **The link between your different activities** — the shop, the forum, and the service can't compare notes to build a profile of you, because each sees a different, unlinkable face.

## The honest limits

We won't pretend this is magic. Three things you should understand:

1. **Someone has to vouch first.** Adelos proves you *hold* a verified claim and that it's *yours* — but the claim has to come from a source the other side trusts (the platform, a school, a government office). Adelos doesn't invent the fact; it carries it, privately.

2. **The voucher knows who you are.** When the social media platform vouched for your adulthood, *it* knew you were you. Your privacy is toward the shop and the wider world — not toward the source that issued the proof in the first place.

3. **It doesn't hide your internet connection.** Adelos protects your identity in the proof itself. It doesn't disguise your IP address or network activity — that's a separate concern.

## The point

Centralised digital ID asks you to be fully known to one authority, and traceable everywhere you go. Adelos lets many different trusted sources vouch for single facts about you, which you then prove to anyone — minimally, privately, and with no one keeping score.

Proven. Disclosed as you choose. Nothing more.
